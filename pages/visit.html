Consumer payments and increasing regulatory requirements.
The need for more transparency [Session details]
Wednesday, October 31
4:00 p.m. – 5:00 p.m.
Community room 2

Consumer cross-border credit transfers are now subject to regulations that require the disclosure of fees, FX rates, and availability, as well as cancellation rights provisions. How should the industry respond? What’s the impact on the open correspondent banking model? What should be market practice? How will “closed loop” providers respond? Hear representatives of leading market infrastructures and the PMPG provide insight into the challenge and share tactics on how to meet regulatory expectations.

Global transaction banking: In need of business insights? [Session details]
Thursday, November 1
11:00 a.m. – 12:00 p.m.
Conference room 2

One of the major consequences of the recent crises on the activities of banks is the fact that global transaction banking is back as a top priority for many of them. This renewed focus of attention takes place in a much more complex and less stable market.

This session will illustrate how analytical products and services can help decision makers of banks to base their decision on the right information, at the right time.
